Mutual Information, Predictive Coding and Lipschitz Continuity

August 2, 2020 in survey

A survey.

1 Mutual Information Neural Estimation, ICML 2018

The mutual information of random variables $X$ and $Z$ is defined as $$ I(X; Z) := H(X) - H(X|Z), \tag{1} $$ where $H$ is the Shannon entropy. The mutual information can be understood as the decrease of the uncertainty in $X$ given $Z$.

Equavalently, we can rewrite $(1)$ in the form of Kullback-Leibler (KL-) divergence between the joint distribution $\mathbb{P}_{XZ}$ and the product of the marginals $\mathbb{P}_X \otimes \mathbb{P}_Z$ :

$$ I(X,Z) = D_{KL}(\mathbb{P}_{XZ} \Vert \mathbb{P}_X \otimes \mathbb{P}_Z) $$

where $D_{KL}$ is defined as

$$ D_{K L}(\mathbb{P} | \mathbb{Q}):=\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{P}}\left[\log \frac{d \mathbb{P}}{d \mathbb{Q}}\right] $$

whenever $\mathbb{P}$ is absolutely continuous w.r.t. $\mathbb{Q}$.

KL-divengence has a dual representation called the Donsker-Varadhan representation, which writes as $$ D_{KL}(\mathbb{P} \Vert \mathbb{Q}) = \sup_{T:\Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}} \mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{P}}[T] - \log(\mathbb{E}_\mathbb{Q}[e^T]), $$ where the supremum is taken over all functions T such that the two expectation are finite. By this dual representation, we can derive a lower bound of KL divergence: $$ D_{KL}(\mathbb{P} \Vert \mathbb{Q}) \ge \sup_{T \in \mathcal{F}} \mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{P}}[T] - \log(\mathbb{E}_\mathbb{Q}[e^T]), $$ where $\mathcal{F}$ is a class of functions satisfing the integrability constraints.

By exploiting this lower bound, cite{} proposed the neural information measure defined as $$ I_{\Theta}(X, Z)=\sup _{\theta \in \Theta} \mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{P}_{X Z}}\left[T_{\theta}\right]-\log \left(\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{P}_{X} \otimes \mathbb{P}_{Z}}\left[e^{T_{\theta}}\right]\right) $$ where $T_\theta$ is parametrized by a deep neural network with parameters $\theta \in \Theta$. We call this network the *statistics network* . In practice, the expectations are estimated using empirical samples from $\mathbb{P}_{XZ}$ and $\mathbb{P}_X \otimes \mathbb{P}_Z$ .
